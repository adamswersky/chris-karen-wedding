The happy couple

A little about the two of them

Between them they can stabilise a patient, explain the Austrian business cycle, and tune a 47-string harp in a draught. They share a flat with wooden floorboards, two fireplaces, home-sewn curtains, stained glass over the doorway, and a coffee grinder that means business.

Christopher

The English one · Economist at heart

Chris approaches life with the methodical curiosity of a man who believes most things are, at root, an allocation problem. He is rarely seen without a wool jumper, holds strong views on monetary policy, and has been known to read the financial press at the breakfast table while maintaining the air of someone enjoying a novel. He also makes excellent coffee, which he considers a form of due diligence.

Economics Bitcoin Finance Wool jumpers Good coffee Quirky

Karen

The Scottish one · Doctor & musician

Karen is a doctor who spends her weekdays keeping people alive and her weekends playing the harp, which is either a study in contrasts or perfect coherence, depending on how you look at it. She is from Scotland, which she regards as a simple fact. She sewed the curtains in their sitting room herself — by hand, beautifully — and has strong opinions about draughts, which is wise given the climate.
